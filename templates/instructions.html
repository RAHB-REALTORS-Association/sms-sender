Instructions

First things first what is Twilio?

Twilio is a platform that offers telecom services in a way that is easy for developers to integrate with applications. These services include SMS, Whatsapp, Voice calls, etc. What they don't offer is a simple interface for people who doesn't know coding to use their services. And that's where this tool comes in. This tools uses your Twilio account to send SMS messages from a simple spreadsheet file. This guide will take you all the way from setting up your Twilio account to delivering your first message. Let's get started!

1 - Getting a Twilio account and credentials go ahead, it's free and doesn't require a credit card

First thing you'll need to do is signup for a Twilio account. Head on over to Twilio's signup page, fill the form and hit "Start your free trial". Once you finish setting up your account you'll be taken to your dashboard. This is where you'll get your Account SID and Token, which we'll use to send your messages. Check the following screenshot: the red boxes show you where these fields are on your dashboard. Notice that your Token won't be visible. Click on the button shown to display your password. Save both the Account SID and Token, as we'll use them shortly.

A word about passwords and security

As stated above, this site doesn't store your credentials in any way. But I wouldn't ask you to just take my word for it. That's why I recommend that you use a Token instead of your Twilio password. This might be a bit advanced for most of the users but if you have some understanding of API security, please follow this article to create a token. You can just use a token on the "Password" field instead of your Twilio password.

2 - Getting a phone number

You'll probably need a phone number on your Twilio account in order to send your messages from. I say probably because there are ways of sending messages without a number. Click here to read more about Alphanumeric Sender IDs. For now, let's stick with a regular number. On your Twilio Dashboard, click on the Hash symbol on the left side of the screen. If it's not visible, expand the menu by clicking on the "three dots" button at the bottom.

On the "Active Numbers" screen, click on the blue "Buy a number" button on the top right corner. Select the country where you want your new number to be and make sure you check the box for "SMS", under Capabilites. This will ensure your new number can send SMS messages. If you cannot find a number for your contry you can go with a US number as they have the best delivery rate anywhere in the world. Once this is done click on the red Search button. Pick a number for from the list and click on "Buy" on the right side. Review the charges and confirm the purchase. Make note of the number as we'll need it when creating the document that will send your messages. That's all we need from Twilio right now. Next we are going to create the list of contacts to which we'll send the messages.

3 - Setting up the list of contacts and message

Now we'll use a spreadsheet software (like Excel) to create a list of contacts and messages that we'll use to send the SMS. I've done this example using Google Sheets but any software will do (Excel, Numbers, LibreOffice, etc):
  1. Open your spreadsheet software and start by entering your Twilio phone number on the first cell (A1).
  2. On the next column (B1) you enter the destination phone number. Make sure to include country and area code.
  3. On the third column (C1) you enter the message you want to send.
That's it! You can copy and paste the message and Twilio phone number to all other rows if they are the same and just fill the B column with your destination phone numbers.

4 - Export the file and upload it to our website

Now you just need to save your spreadsheet on the format our site can use and upload it to us. How to do that might differ a bit from app to app:

  • On Google Sheets click on "File", "Download", "Comma-separated values (.csv, current sheet)
  • On Excel
    • click on "File" and then in "Save as". On the "File Format" box select "Comma Separated Values (.csv)"
  • On Numbers for Macclick on "File", "Export to", "CSV" and proceed to save the file
  • On OpenOffice
    • click on "File", "Save as" and set "File type" as "Text CSV (.csv)"
Once you have you file ready go back to our home page and use the form at the bottom to upload your file and Twilio credentials. That's it! If everything is alright we'll start sending your messages and once we are done we'll show you a full report, so please be patient!
